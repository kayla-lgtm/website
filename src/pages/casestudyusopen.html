Case Study

The US Open is a smarter business

Harnessing technology to drive fan engagement with efficiency, flexibility and innovation.

Challenge

For three weeks at the end of summer, more than one million people travel to Flushing, New York, to watch the world’s best tennis players compete in the US Open Tennis Championships. But the in-person crowd is only part of the story. More than 14 million global tennis fans follow the tournament through the US Open app and website.

To keep fans engaged, the United States Tennis Association (USTA) has partnered with IBM for more than three decades to design, develop, and continuously evolve a world-class digital experience. As fan expectations increased, the USTA needed to constantly innovate, anticipate new behaviors, and deliver richer experiences at scale.

The challenge was operational as much as experiential. The US Open is a global digital operation that must respond in real time to demand spikes, shifting audience expectations, and the need for nonstop innovation while maintaining reliability and performance.

Solution

IBM Consulting worked side by side with the USTA digital team to transform massive volumes of US Open data into content and insight that informs and engages fans. This was enabled through a smarter-business architecture built on hybrid cloud, AI, data, and automation.

Hybrid Cloud: The US Open runs on a hybrid multicloud architecture using Red Hat OpenShift for containerization. This lets the USTA write applications once and run them across environments, helping them scale rapidly, launch new capabilities faster, and handle traffic spikes that can exceed 5,000% during high-interest matches.

Data: Each point played generates more than 150 data points. Across the tournament, this adds up to over 7 million data points, plus 20+ years of historical match data and millions of media articles. The USTA uses IBM watsonx.data to centralize and curate this information in a hybrid, open data lakehouse so AI models can generate real-time fan insights.

AI: With IBM watsonx.ai, watsonx Orchestrate, and IBM Granite models, the USTA embedded AI directly into editorial workflows. AI-generated commentary, article summaries, and contextual insights are produced at scale to deepen fan engagement throughout the tournament.

Automation: The USTA automated critical IT operations using IBM Instana for observability, HashiCorp Terraform for infrastructure provisioning, and IBM Apptio for cloud cost governance across thousands of resources.

The AI-powered fan features in the US Open app include Match Chat, Key Points summaries, and Live Likelihood to Win, delivering richer context and more interactive experiences during and after matches.

Results

The US Open digital platform now delivers more than 7 million AI-generated insights during the tournament, while supporting an expected 14+ million unique users in 2025.

Editorial productivity improved dramatically. In 2024, USTA editors increased first-round Match Reports from 20 to 64, representing more than a 300% productivity gain.

Automation reduced manual build and provisioning cycle time by 80%, and the platform achieved 99.999% uptime during tournament play.

Together, these outcomes created a scalable digital foundation that continuously improves fan engagement while giving the USTA flexibility to innovate year after year.
